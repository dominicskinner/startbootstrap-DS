About

Investigating Neurologic Diseases

I'm interested in neurodegenerative diseases and the intersection between neuroscience, immunology and virology.

I investigate the molecular and cellular pathways of disease progression using the mouse coronavirus (JHMV) as a model of Multiple Sclerosis. Specifically, I look at stimulating remyelination and how the innate immune system and glial cells contribute to disease progression.

Recently, our lab has expanded into SARs-cov-2 research partnering with several biotech companies to test anti viral therapies in both our mouse coronavirus model and the novel coronavirus. I have also undergone BSL3/aBSL3 training to conduct in vivo and in vitro experiments with the novel coronavirus and how it affects the central nervous system in the near future.

Experience with Single Cell RNA-seq, ELISA, Immunohistochemistry, PCR, qPCR, Multi-color Flow cytometry, Plaque Assays, RNA/DNA isolation, R, BSL2, BSL3/aBSL3 and more.
